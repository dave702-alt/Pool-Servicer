⚠ Pebble Tec rules: Never use stainless steel brush for regular brushing. Never over-shock (fades color). Always keep alkalinity 80–120 ppm (low alk etches surface). Calcium hardness 200–400 ppm. Your Dec 2025 reading of 600 ppm is high — scale inhibitor is essential.
Weekly brushing
Nylon brush only — every week
Nylon bristle brush only — never stainless steel for regular use. Brush walls and floor toward the main drain weekly. The textured pebble surface traps dirt and algae spores in its crevices — weekly brushing prevents algae without extra chemicals. Start at the steps (most vulnerable area).
Your #1 threat: calcium scaling
600 ppm hardness is very high
Your Dec 2025 service note logged 600 ppm total hardness. Target is 200–400 ppm. CA hard water + Pebble Tec + high evaporation = scaling risk. Use a scale inhibitor monthly. Without it you'll get the white calcium haze on the surface and the white ring at the waterline tile. This is your biggest maintenance risk.
Water chemistry (Pebble Tec specific)
Tighter targets than standard plaster
Chlorine: 1–3 ppm (lower than standard to protect color)
pH: 7.4–7.6 — yours runs 7.6–8.0, fix weekly
Alkalinity: 80–120 ppm — low alk etches the finish
Calcium hardness: 200–400 ppm
CYA: 40–60 ppm — yours is 60–70, don't add more
Acid wash schedule
Every 2–4 years — pro job
Drain and acid wash every 2–4 years to remove calcium buildup, algae staining, and the white ring at the waterline. This is especially important in California with hard water. This is a professional job — not DIY. Budget ~$400–600 when the time comes.
Algae prevention
Pebble Tec needs more vigilance
The textured surface gives algae more surface area to grip vs smooth plaster. Your mineral pack (Apr + Oct) is critical — don't skip it. If you see green starting, brush immediately with a nylon brush and shock — but don't over-shock (fades the color). For active algae: a combo 50/50 nylon/stainless brush can be used to scrub problem areas only.
What damages Pebble Tec
Avoid these
❌ Stainless brush for regular use
❌ Over-shocking — fades the color
❌ Low alkalinity — etches the cement matrix
❌ Chlorine into skimmer — discolors floor around drains
❌ Concentrated chlorine sitting on surface when pump is off
❌ Ignoring phosphates — feeds algae in textured surface
Lifespan: With proper maintenance, Pebble Tec lasts 20+ years. Main threats: poor chemistry (low alk + high calcium), over-shocking, and stainless steel brushes. Your mineral pack twice a year + weekly nylon brushing are the biggest DIY wins for longevity.  Official Pebble Tec Care Guide →
